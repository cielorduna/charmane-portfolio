About Me

A psych grad who
designs for people,
not just screens.

I graduated with a degree in Psychology, which gave me something most designers pick up later: a real interest in how people think, what they notice first, and what makes them take action.

That background eventually pointed me toward web design. I realized that a good website isn't just about aesthetics — it's about making things easy for the person looking at the screen. That's something I find genuinely interesting to work on.

I build websites using Duda, and I have a solid grasp of on-page SEO — so the sites I build aren't just good-looking, they're also set up to be found. I enjoy the process from start to finish: understanding the goal, organizing the structure, and building something clean that works on any device.

I'm at the start of my design career, and I'm looking for a team where I can keep learning and contribute right away.

Psychology background

Understanding user behavior isn't a bonus skill — it's baked in. I think about who's reading the page before I design it.

Clean, purposeful layouts

I don't fill space for the sake of it. Every section has a reason to be there, and the layout guides visitors without them noticing.

SEO-aware by default

Heading structure, page titles, and metadata aren't afterthoughts. I build them into the site from the beginning.

Easy to work with

I communicate clearly, ask the right questions early, and know how to stay organized across a project from brief to launch.
